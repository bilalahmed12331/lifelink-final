About blood donation

One donation. Up to three lives.

Whole blood is separated into red cells, platelets and plasma — each going to a different patient. A single visit, roughly an hour of your time, can support a surgery patient, a cancer patient in treatment and a newborn all at once.

  • Safe, screened, and supervised by medical staff
  • Takes about 45–60 minutes start to finish
  • Your body replaces the donated volume within days

Who can donate?

Most healthy adults aged 18–65, weighing over 50kg, can donate every 90 days. Eligibility depends on recent illness, medication and travel history — our AI Assistant inside the donor dashboard can walk you through it.

Why donate

Good for them. Good for you, too.

Donating blood is one of the few acts of generosity that also benefits the giver.

Saves lives directly

Every unit donated can help patients facing surgery, trauma, childbirth complications or chronic illness.

Free mini health check

Every donation includes a quick check of pulse, blood pressure, haemoglobin and temperature.

Supports heart health

Regular donation is linked with healthier iron levels and reduced strain on the cardiovascular system.

Strengthens community

Donors become part of a standing network hospitals can call on the moment an urgent request comes in.

Whole blood donation is typically safe every 90 days for men and every 120 days for women, giving your body time to fully replenish iron stores.
